Neighbours Getting Together

There are so many benefits to connecting with your neighbours. Here are four of our favourites. Street, apartment block, or community get-togethers are a great way to get to know your neighbours and also work towards making your community safer, stronger and resilient in case of emergency.



Neighbourliness is good for NZ’s communities. Chippin in’s goal is to gather neighbours together for fish ’n’ chips and discuss fire safety. We are wanting to make people more aware about fire safety and how to deal with it as a community.

Social connection is good for our wellbeing. So come along to our fish n’ chips event and meet your neighbours. We’ll supply the food or you can order what you want for the event.

Fire Safety

1. RESCUE: Make sure to alert everyone in the building. Help each other make your way to the meeting place agreed in advance. Check that everyone has made it out.

2. ALARM: Notify the fire service on emergency number 111. Give the exact address. You should also teach children how to do this. Do not hang up until you are told to do so. The fire service is already on its way, while you are speaking.

3. EXTINGUISH: If the fire is not too large, try to put it out. Do not expose yourself or others to significant danger. Remember that smoke is very toxic
